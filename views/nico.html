Overview

Lasagna alla Bolognese

  • Italian
  • Italian food
  • Bolognese
  • Proudly Italian

Lasagne alla Bolognese is an institution, typical for Sunday lunches. This rich and tasty dish originates from the Emilia region, specifically, from the city of Bologna. Lasagna, however, is known, appreciated, made, remade, tasted and shared not only throughout Italy, but also abroad as an Italian symbolic dish.

100 unsurpassed!

Prep: 120 minutes; Cook: 180 minutes.

Method

How to cook

  1. Take a large 12x8 inches (30x20 cm) lasagna pan and grease the bottom. Add a thin layer of béchamel and one of Bolognese sauce. Then place the first sheet.
  2. add a layer of béchamel and a layer of meat sauce. Add grated Parmesan cheese and place another sheet of pasta on top.
  3. Add another layer of béchamel and one of meat sauce, add grated cheese and you can add prosciutto cotto as well, continue like this until you have 5 layers. After placing the last sheet, add the Bolognese sauce.
  4. in order to completely cover the pasta sheet. Sprinkle with grated cheese, add some tufts of butter and bake in a preheated static oven at 338°F (170°C) for 40 minutes.
  5. Tips for the Bolognese: In the past, milk was also used to dampen the wild taste of meat during cooking, but it is something that is no longer or rarely done today. There are those who use a mix of pork and beef to get a fat mix at the right point. We advise you to use, in addition to pancetta bacon, skirt stea or in any case a cut that is not too lean.
