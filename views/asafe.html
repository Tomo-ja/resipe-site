Overview

Pastel

  • Brazil
  • Brazilian Food
  • "Pastel de feira"
  • "Traditional Food

Pastel is a food composed of a dough based on flour that is formed in an envelope, filled and then fried by soaking in boiling oil. Originating from European cuisine, pastel is one of the most frequently found foods in street carts and popular trade centers in Brazil

15 portions

30 min

Ingredients

Ingredients

3 cups of wheat flour

1 cup of warm water

3 tablespoons (soup) of oil

1 tablespoons (soup) of cachaça

1 tablespoons (soup) of salt

More wheat flour to work the dough

Method

How to cook

  1. Place the flour mixed with the salt in a bowl or table and open a hole in the middle.
  2. In this hole, place the oil, the cachaça and some water.
  3. Mix the water and flour gradually, each time picking up a little more flour from the edge of the hole.
  4. The dough should be soft..
  5. If it's a little sticky, it's okay, If it is too sticky, add more flour.
  6. If it's hard, put some more water in it.
  7. On a floured surface, open the dough with the aid of a roll, so that it is very thin.(If it doesn't get thin, it won't get crispy after frying.)
  8. Fill to taste, and close with a fork or with the back of a knife.
  9. Fry in hot oil (not too much) over medium-high heat and drain with the aid of a skimbag, before leaving to dry on absorbent paper.
