Overview

Nikujaga(肉じゃが)

  • Japanese
  • Main
  • Meat
  • Potato

We are making Nikujaga, one of the most popular dishes in home cooking. This time, we will add a minimum amount of water to bring out the maximum flavor from the ingredients. The combined umami of the meat and vegetable makes this dish absolutely delicious!

100

30 min

Ingredients

Ingredients

Thin Beef Slices

Potatoes

Carrot

Onion

Green Peas

Soy sauce

Sugar

Sake

Dashi stock

Method

How to cook

  1. First, let's prepare the ingredients. Cut the potato into relatively large bite-size pieces. Lightly rinse the potato and wipe the excess water from the surface.
  2. Cut the carrot into bite-size pieces while slightly rotating it for each cut.
  3. Cut the onion in half lengthwise and slice it into 4 wedges along the grain, leaving the bottom part still attached.
  4. Remove the firm stringy part from the snow pea pods. Pull the string from the stem end to the tip along the pod.
  5. Let's make Nikujaga. Heat about a half tablespoonful of vegetable oil in a pan. Arrange the beef slices on the pan.
  6. When the redness in the beef almost turns brown, transfer all of the beef to a tray.
  7. Now, in the same pan, add the potato and carrot and stir-fry.
  8. Thoroughly stir-fry until the surface of the potato becomes translucent. Cooking the ingredients thoroughly at this stage helps to reduce the water you will add later.
  9. Distribute the saved beef onto the potato. Pour over the dashi stock.
  10. Add the sugar and sake. Submerge the sugar in the broth and lightly stir to dissolve it.
  11. Cover the ingredients with a paper towel. This will work as an otoshibuta, also known as a drop-lid, and help cook the ingredients with a smaller amount of broth.
  12. Cover with a lid that fits the pan well and simmer for 10 minutes. When you check the heat, listen to the sound at a close distance. You will hear the pleasant simmering sound at the desired heat level. If the heat is too strong, the potato will break apart as the broth boils.
  13. When you have simmered for 10 minutes, move the paper towel to the side and pour over the soy sauce.
  14. After simmer for 10 minutes more, skewer the potato with a bamboo stick to check if it is cooked through.
  15. Finally, arrange the ingredients and garnish with the saved snow peas. This nikujaga is colorful, visually appealing and delicious!
